Crime in the United States

These metrics are per-capita, that is, the crimes in each state is divided by the population of that state.
Puerto Rico is not in the FBI crime dataset, therefore, it is always NaN for all of these maps.
All of the data here was taken from the FBI's Crime in the United States 2019


Total Crime

Total crime appears to be strongest in South Dakota, Kentucky, and Mississippi, peaking in Illinois with 56.602 crimes per 1000 persons.


Burglaries

Burglaries... in Louisiana and Nevada? Louisiana makes some sense, I have heard of tons of property being stolen after hurricanes hit Louisiana.
It is highest in Louisiana with 1.089 burglaries per 1000 persons.
California also makes sense, there are a lot of problems over there.
But Nevada, I don't know. And Alaska I'm also left clueless. Alaska? Really?


Fraud

Most frauds are caught in... Delaware, with 1.352 frauds caught for every 1000 persons in Delaware.
This makes sense since Delaware has some special tax loopholes that a lot of businesses take advantage of, so I would
guess that a lot of the frauds are related to those businesses.


Gambling

What's this? It appears that most gambling crimes occur in... Hawaii? I have no answer for this.
